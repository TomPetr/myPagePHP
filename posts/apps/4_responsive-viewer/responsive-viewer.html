Responsive Image Viewer

Screen is Spinning, Everything is Changing

As part of my thumbnail image gallery, I wanted visitors to be able to see an image they selected displayed in kind of responsive image viewer window. I wanted the viewer’s window height to take only certain portion of the browser window’s heigh so it would be big enough to show displayed image in detail, but at the time not too big as it shouldn’t take over the entire viewport.

The viewer’s sizes and width-to-height ratio need to response to specific screen proportions, which may by changed any time when users resizes browser window by rotating their device or resizing browser window on their desktops. And at the same time, each image may have different and unique proportions and orientation. Beside being either portrait or landscape oriented, or simply squared, they can be also very narrow and tall, or very narrow and long. And anything in between. There are too many variables at play, but this code will cover them all.
